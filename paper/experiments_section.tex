\section{Experimental Studies}
\label{sec:experiments}

% NOTE: This section is written for the main paper. It emphasizes the empirical
% claims and defers implementation details to the appendix file
% paper/experiments_appendix.tex.

\paragraph{Setup.}
We test the theory in a frozen-teacher linear-Gaussian setting where the ground
truth predictive subspace is known. Each example has latent state
$s\sim\mathcal N(0,I_{r_\star})$, context
$z=Cs+\sigma_z\epsilon_z$, and stacked targets
$Y=(y_1^\top,\ldots,y_K^\top)^\top$ with
$y_k=A_ks+\sigma_y\epsilon_k$. The context map $C$ is fixed, so the experiments
isolate the effect of target structure, bottleneck rank, and finite-sample
conditioning. For rank-growth diagnostics we use the stacked covariance
$\Sigma_{YZ}$; for bottleneck and post-saturation diagnostics we use the
whitened raw predictor $T_K=\Sigma_{YZ}^{(K)}\Sigma_{ZZ}^{-1/2}$.

\paragraph{Models.}
We compare the unconstrained ordinary least-squares predictor with its
rank-$d$ reduced-rank regression (RRR) truncation. The RRR rank $d$ plays the
role of the JEPA embedding bottleneck. This removes optimization and encoder
dynamics, letting each experiment target one mechanism from the theory.

\paragraph{Data.}
The target stack is constructed to separate complementarity, capacity, and
conditioning. We use ten deterministic seeds and report means with
standard-error bands. \Cref{tab:experiment-summary} summarizes the main
diagnostics; exact generator details and appendix-only controls are in
\cref{app:experimental-details}.

\paragraph{Metrics.}
All paper-facing risk panels use the average per-target normalization of
$\mathcal R_K$ in \eqref{eq:per_target_risk}, so risks are comparable when
$K$ changes. We report the quantities appearing in the theory: recovered rank,
subspace error, heterogeneity $\lambda_{\mathrm{het}}$, relative conditioning
$\kappa_K^2$, effective dimension, and spectral-tail ratios. Population values
computed from the known operators are shown as dashed curves; finite-sample
estimates are shown as solid curves. The appendix defines the effective-rank
thresholds, fixed-reference-stack probes, calibrated constants, and
Wedin-bound diagnostics.

\paragraph{Baselines.}
The main baseline is the population counterpart of each finite-sample metric:
it is computed directly from the known operators, without sampling noise. For
bottleneck risk, unconstrained OLS is the reference model and the predicted
floor is the spectral tail beyond the retained rank.

\paragraph{Results.}
\Cref{fig:k-saturation,fig:heterogeneity,fig:bottleneck} validate the
pre-saturation and bottleneck mechanisms. Complementary targets increase
recovered rank and lower per-target MSE until rank saturates. Larger measured
$\widehat{\lambda}_{\mathrm{het}}$ improves finite-sample recovery. Sweeping
$d$ confirms the capacity prediction: model rank follows
$\min\{d,r_\star\}$ and excess risk tracks the spectral-tail floor.

\Cref{fig:post-saturation} isolates the second, finite-sample saturation
mechanism. Here $r_\star(K)=d$ throughout, so additional targets cannot create
new rank. They still help when they improve the relative conditioning
$\kappa_K^2=\lambda_d(H_K)/\lambda_1(H_K)$ of the already accessible predictive
directions. As $\kappa_K^2$ increases, subspace error, excess risk, and
finite-sample risk decrease; the half-gap and calibrated-bound checks are
reported in \cref{app:experimental-details}.

\Cref{fig:predictive-isotropy} tests the fixed-trace isotropy prediction. With
rank and $\operatorname{tr}(H_K)$ fixed, only the eigenvalue profile of
$H_K=T_K^\top T_K$ changes. Consistent with
\cref{lem:predictive_isotropy,thm:predictive_isotropy_opt}, flatter spectra
increase the weakest retained signal relative to perturbation, reduce
$\|\sin\Theta_T\|_{\mathrm{op}}$, and lower the balanced-reference-stack excess
MSE. Appendix diagnostics show that this predictive isotropy is distinct from
embedding isotropy, and that the gauge-factorization claims in
\cref{subsubsec:gauge_factorization} hold under both Gaussian and non-Gaussian
contexts.
Finally, \cref{fig:regularizer-digits-samples} visualizes the real-image
digit-half prediction task, and \cref{tab:regularizer-digits-cost} reports
the corresponding ten-seed quantitative diagnostic. The proposed
prediction-aware regularizer reduces low-label target-half pixel MSE and the
train--test gap while also improving the prediction-side Gaussianity and
covariance-balance diagnostics.
